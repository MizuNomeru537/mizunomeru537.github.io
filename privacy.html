Privacy Policy

ToolLab is designed around lightweight browser tools. Many common tasks can be completed locally in the browser without requiring account creation or persistent storage on a server.

1. Information handled on the site

Most tools on ToolLab are built to process input directly in the browser. That means common actions such as formatting JSON, generating UUIDs, converting text, or comparing two text blocks can often be completed without sending personal content to a dedicated application backend.

2. Information you choose to enter

If you paste text, code, or other data into a tool, that information is handled for the purpose of operating the feature you selected. Users should avoid entering confidential information into public online tools unless they understand the risk and are comfortable doing so.

3. Cookies, advertising, and measurement

The site may use advertising or basic measurement services that rely on cookies or similar technologies. These services can help operate the site, understand broad usage patterns, and support monetization. Third-party services may process technical information according to their own policies.

4. External links and third-party content

Some pages may link to external resources, documentation, or platforms. Once you leave ToolLab, the privacy practices of those external sites are controlled by their own operators.

5. Data minimization approach

  • No account is required for normal use of the site.
  • Tools are designed to stay simple and avoid collecting unnecessary user data.
  • Where a task can be handled locally in the browser, that approach is preferred.

6. Updates to this policy

This policy can be updated as the site changes, including when new tools, analytics, or communication channels are added.

If you use browser utilities for work, avoid pasting secrets, passwords, internal credentials, or private personal information into any public site unless you fully trust the workflow.
